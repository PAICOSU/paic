Classification of Grass Seed Crop Species using Spaceborne Imagery and Artificial Intelligence-Driven Models

🛰

Project Overview

The Oregon Willamette Valley is the premier grass seed producing region in the world, growing dozens of cool-season grass species including ryegrasses, fescues, and bluegrasses. Accurately mapping the spatial distribution of these species is essential for agricultural statistics, supply chain management, pest monitoring, and regional planning. This project develops AI-driven classification models trained on multi-temporal satellite imagery to produce high-accuracy, field-scale crop type maps.

Objectives

  • Compile multi-temporal, multi-spectral satellite imagery time series from platforms including Sentinel-2 and Planet
  • Develop ground truth datasets through field surveys and collaboration with Oregon seed growers
  • Train, validate, and compare machine learning and deep learning classifiers (Random Forest, CNN, Transformer-based models) for species discrimination
  • Produce annual crop type maps and assess temporal consistency and transferability across growing seasons

Methods

Google Earth Engine (GEE) provides the cloud computing infrastructure for accessing and preprocessing large satellite image archives. Time-series feature extraction captures the distinct phenological trajectories of different grass seed species across the growing season. Training data integration from field-collected GPS points and digitized parcel boundaries ensures spatially accurate model supervision. Accuracy assessments include confusion matrices, producer/user accuracy, and spatial uncertainty mapping.

Impact

Accurate, annual grass seed crop maps will support the Oregon Department of Agriculture, the Oregon Seed Council, and individual growers with better inventory estimates, logistics planning, and response to pest or disease outbreaks. This work also advances the broader field of fine-scale crop type mapping in complex, mixed agricultural landscapes.

Key Details

  • Data: Sentinel-2, Planet satellite imagery
  • Platform: Google Earth Engine
  • Methods: Deep learning, time-series analysis
  • Region: Willamette Valley, Oregon
  • Status: Active
