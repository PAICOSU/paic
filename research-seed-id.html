Seed Identification

🌿

Project Overview

Accurate seed identification is fundamental to seed quality assurance, varietal purity certification, and plant breeding programs. Traditional seed identification relies on time-consuming manual inspection by trained analysts. This project develops rapid, high-throughput machine learning systems capable of automatically identifying and classifying seeds based on their visual morphological characteristics captured through imaging.

Objectives

  • Develop a comprehensive labeled image database of seed species relevant to Pacific Northwest agriculture
  • Train and optimize convolutional neural network (CNN) classifiers for species-level and variety-level identification
  • Investigate the use of 3D imaging and hyperspectral imaging as complementary data sources
  • Build a prototype field-deployable seed identification tool for industry and regulatory use

Methods

Seeds are imaged using high-resolution flatbed scanners, macro photography rigs, and structured light systems to capture precise morphological features including size, shape, color, surface texture, and embryo characteristics. Feature extraction methods range from traditional morphometric analysis to deep representation learning, enabling robust classification even for visually similar varieties.

Significance

Rapid and reliable seed identification supports the Oregon seed industry by reducing time and cost of quality testing, enabling faster varietal release in breeding programs, and improving regulatory compliance in seed certification. The AI tools developed will be applicable across a broad range of crop species beyond the initial focus taxa.

Key Details

  • Focus: Grass seed species & varieties
  • Methods: Machine learning, imaging, morphometrics
  • Applications: Quality assurance, breeding, certification
  • Status: Active
